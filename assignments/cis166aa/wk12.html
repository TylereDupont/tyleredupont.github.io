How can you stay safe

Font Size



Keep software up to date

It is very important to update your web browser, and operating system whenever possible. These updates contain crucial vulnerability updates, and are your best way to stay safe online.

Create secure passwords

Creating a secure password will not help you if you have installed a keylogger (Software that keeps track of every key you press and sends it to another source) however it will keep you secure from a brute force attack, (A brute force attack is when someone uses a program to randomly guess your password thousands of times.) The best passwords contain letters, caps, numbers, and symbols. (ex. wOOfb@rk!5782)

Keep your anonymity

You need to be extremely careful about what information you give out (to a person, or program.) Scammers online will often impersonate somebody else such as a “virus removal specialist” and will ask for your information and attempt to make you install software on your computer which they can use to gather information about you (Account logins, credit card information, etc.) Remember that anybody can be anybody on the internet, and don’t just give away your information or install programs onto your computer without consulting a professional.

Keep your network secure

When at home or work your password protected router will keep your device, and information safe, however public Wi-Fi connections are often unsecured, leaving your device and information at risk. To stay safe you should install a VPN (Virtual private network.) A VPN will keep your connection secure, and hide your unique Ip address.

Know the latest scams

Be sure to stay up to date on the latest scams going on. A very popular software right now is called Ransomware. If you are tricked into installing Ransomware, an attacker will encrypt all the files on your computer and will not unlock them unless you send money via bitcoin or other cryptocurrencies.

Selective downloading

Only download software from sources that you trust such as school, or work. Software from an unknown source could be anything. It could be keeping track of your keypresses and may attempt to hack into your online banking portal, or it may be a bitcoin miner (a piece of software that performs calculations using your pc hardware to earn somebody else money. In short, a bitcoin miner will drastically slow down your pc)

Hover suspicious links

If you hover your mouse over a link, you will be shown the actual destination on the bottom left of your browser. If it looks suspicious or illegitimate in any way, don’t click it.

Keep your connection secure

If a website is secure, http in the address bar will change to https, and you will also see a small padlock icon. If a website is not secure, it may be trying to gather your credit card information for resale on the dark net.
